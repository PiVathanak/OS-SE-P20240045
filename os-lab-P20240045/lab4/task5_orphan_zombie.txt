--- Zombie and Orphan Cleanup Notes ---
Q: How are orphans cleaned up? A: Orphan processes are adopted by init/systemd (PID 1), which later calls wait() to clean them up.
Q: How are zombies cleaned up? A: Zombies are cleaned when the parent calls wait() or waitpid(); if the parent dies, init/systemd adopts and cleans them.
Q: Can you kill a zombie with kill -9? Why or why not? A: No, because a zombie is already dead and only exists in the process table; signals have no effect.
